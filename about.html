For over two and a half decades, Viento has sculpted the unseen poetry of spaces. Born in Mumbai's golden glow, our story began with a single vertical blind—a humble stroke in a canvas of light. In the quiet of the 1990s, we worked with only a handful of shades, our hands learning the language of fabric and sun. Back then, every texture was imported, every design borrowed. But we saw what others didn’t: the potential to redefine the very fabric of window coverings.

The Evolution

  • The Early Years: We mastered the alchemy of light filtration first with blinds, then through whispers of innovation. Slowly, relentlessly, we expanded: new materials, new geometries, new cities. Mumbai's chaos became our muse.
  • The Turning Point: Relationships with artisans and vendors across India became the bedrock of our craft. No longer content with imports, we dreamed of our own thread, our own patterns, our own signature.
  • The Revelation: In the late 2020s, Viento's first factory rose in Sarigam, Gujarat a sanctuary of self-reliance. Here, we began weaving our own fabrics, cutting ties with dependency, and writing our own design manifesto.
  • The Expansion: Bangalore became our southern ode in 2020, a second home for innovation. By then, our fabrics were not just materials—they were heirlooms, supplied to makers across the country.

At Viento, we don’t just make blinds—we reimagine the way light lives inside your home. Light has the power to shift a mood, define a moment, and shape the character of a space. Our mission is to give you mastery over that power—not through bulky mechanisms or limited materials, but through thoughtfully engineered textiles that respond to light with precision and purpose. We believe that window treatments should do more than block or blur—they should enhance, elevate, and enrich your experience of a room.

That’s why every Viento fabric is designed at the intersection of material science and interior aesthetics. From blackout to sheer, each weave, thread, and finish is carefully calibrated to interact with sunlight in specific ways—whether it’s soft diffusion for warmth, complete darkness for rest, or elegant transitions throughout the day. We’re not just filtering sunlight—we’re crafting textiles that command it. Because when you master light, you don’t just change how a room looks. You change how it feels.

At the heart of our craft is a relentless pursuit of harmony—between light and shadow, material and mood, innovation and intimacy. We collaborate closely with designers, architects, and dreamers to ensure that every Viento blind is not just a product, but an integral part of a spatial experience. Our mission is not to follow trends, but to set new standards for what fabric, form, and function can achieve together.

We envision a future where architecture is not just designed—it’s redefined. A future where form follows feeling, and buildings become more than shelters; they become experiences. At Indochine, we believe great architecture doesn’t just stand—it speaks. It listens. It adapts. And most of all, it transforms the way we live, breathe, and see light.

In this vision, every window is more than glass. It’s a sculptor of light, a storyteller of the sun. We design with the belief that light is not a passive element to be managed, but an active force to be shaped—bent, softened, sharpened—until it aligns perfectly with the soul of the space. To redefine architecture is to embrace this mastery—where materials serve emotion, and every opening bends light to its will.

We dream of spaces where light becomes choreography—shifting, flowing, pausing—at the touch of a hand or the turn of the sun. In this future, architecture responds like nature does: fluid, alive, and in rhythm with its inhabitants. Our vision is to lead that movement, blending textile engineering with architectural sensitivity to create structures that don’t just occupy space—they elevate it.

We see a world where architecture no longer fights the environment—it partners with it. Where light is not merely managed, but invited, sculpted, and celebrated. At Indochine, our vision is rooted in this symbiosis—crafting spaces that breathe with the day, shift with the seasons, and glow with purpose. It's not just about creating buildings, but crafting atmospheres that feel intuitively alive—where light dances, silence speaks, and design becomes destiny.

"The Viento Philosophy"

Viento is not merely seen—it is felt. Like the wind that leaves no trace yet moves mountains, our work exists in the delicate space between presence and absence. It is in the hush between sunlight and shadow, in the quiet confidence of forms that speak without needing to be loud.

We are alchemists of the intangible—shaping air, light, and feeling into something visible, touchable, unforgettable. Every stroke, every curve, every fabric is chosen not for its opulence alone, but for its ability to stir emotion. We believe luxury lies not in excess, but in restraint—in details that whisper instead of shout.

This is the elegance of stillness. The softness that commands presence. The beauty that lingers long after you've left the room. This is Viento—where design meets poetry, and where quiet magnificence becomes a way of life.
